# FlōGolf System Pitch

Prepared March 12, 2026 based on FlōGolf's public website, program pages, and public listings.

## Core Pitch

FlōGolf does not have a demand problem. It has a systems problem.

The venue already has a strong business: simulator bookings, memberships, lessons, leagues, private events, food and drink, and gift cards. The issue is that those revenue lines appear to run across fragmented tools, manual staff follow-up, and disconnected customer flows.

The pitch is:

> I can build the FlōGolf System: an internal operating layer that connects bookings, lessons, memberships, leagues, events, follow-up, and reporting into one system so you generate more revenue, lose fewer customers, reduce manual work, and have a foundation that can scale across multiple locations.

## What Makes This The Right Pitch

Selling "automation" alone is too small.

FlōGolf's actual opportunity is bigger:

- consolidate fragmented workflows into one operating system
- own the customer journey from first visit to repeat revenue
- reduce dependence on staff memory and manual coordination
- create one source of truth for customer data, activity, and revenue
- make expansion to future locations operationally realistic

That is more valuable than offering a few isolated automations because it addresses the root issue instead of treating symptoms one by one.

## What The Website Suggests Today

Based on the public site, FlōGolf appears to monetize across:

1. Bay bookings
2. Memberships
3. Lessons
4. Leagues and tournaments
5. Private events
6. Food, drinks, and gift cards

That is a healthy business model, but it also means the backend needs to coordinate multiple customer journeys well.

The current site strongly suggests fragmentation:

- Membership activation appears to require customers to contact support after purchase.
- Lesson purchases appear to require manual scheduling follow-up after checkout.
- League coordination includes weekly pairing and ongoing communication.
- Private events run through a separate event request and Tripleseat flow.
- Indexed `flogolflounge.com` and `www.flogolflounge.com` pages show conflicting pricing, hours, and offer details.
- Some public pages appear duplicated, stale, or inconsistent.

That combination usually means revenue is being managed across too many disconnected surfaces.

## The Real Business Problem

When a business like FlōGolf runs on fragmented tools, the losses usually show up in predictable places:

- leads do not get followed up fast enough
- staff manually bridge gaps between systems
- customers drop off between purchase and activation
- lessons and memberships underperform because nurture is inconsistent
- event leads go cold
- reporting is unreliable because customer activity is spread across multiple platforms
- opening a second location becomes more complex than it should be

That is the heart of the pitch:

> FlōGolf is already selling multiple high-value offers. The problem is that those offers do not appear to be managed through one internal system built to maximize conversion, retention, and scale.

## The FlōGolf System

The FlōGolf System would be the internal operating layer behind the business.

It would not need to replace every tool on day one. The smarter approach is to centralize data, workflows, and reporting first, then decide what should be integrated, upgraded, or replaced over time.

At a high level, the FlōGolf System would unify:

- customer profiles
- bookings
- memberships
- lessons
- league operations
- event sales
- gift card lifecycle
- communications
- reporting

## What The FlōGolf System Would Do

### 1. Centralize Customer Data

Every guest interaction should feed one profile:

- first bay booking
- lesson purchase
- membership status
- league participation
- event inquiry
- gift card purchase or redemption
- visit history
- spend history

This creates one source of truth for the business instead of scattered platform data.

### 2. Run The Full Customer Lifecycle

The system should manage the full arc of the customer relationship:

- first inquiry
- first visit
- repeat booking
- upsell into lessons or memberships
- league participation
- event booking
- reactivation if they go cold

That turns FlōGolf from a series of transactions into a managed revenue engine.

### 3. Remove Manual Operational Gaps

The current public flows suggest several places where staff likely step in manually.

The system should remove that by handling:

- membership activation
- lesson intake and scheduling
- booking reminders and arrival instructions
- league reminders and status updates
- event lead follow-up
- renewal and win-back campaigns

The goal is not just convenience. It is reducing customer loss between steps.

### 4. Give Full Operational Control

A custom internal system gives FlōGolf more control over:

- customer data
- offer structure
- workflows
- communications
- reporting
- location-level operations

That matters because fragmented SaaS tools are convenient early, but they become limiting when the business wants tighter execution or expansion.

### 5. Create A Multi-Location Foundation

This is one of the strongest parts of the pitch.

If FlōGolf opens more locations, the problem gets worse without an internal system. More venues means:

- more bookings
- more staff
- more customer records
- more event pipelines
- more membership complexity
- more reporting needs

The FlōGolf System would create the structure to support:

- location-specific inventory and schedules
- centralized customer records across locations
- shared reporting
- standardized workflows
- cross-location promotions and memberships

That makes scale possible without multiplying operational mess.

## Concrete Improvements The System Would Drive

### Bookings

- automate confirmations, reminders, and arrival instructions
- reduce no-shows
- prompt repeat bookings
- upsell guests into off-peak sessions, lessons, or memberships

### Lessons

- remove email back-and-forth after purchase
- route lesson buyers into intake and scheduling automatically
- track package usage
- trigger follow-up and retention campaigns

### Memberships

- activate instantly after purchase
- deliver member access instructions automatically
- track perk usage
- improve renewals and win-backs

### Leagues

- automate registration confirmations and reminders
- manage pairings and communications
- publish standings and recap updates
- drive re-enrollment next season

### Events

- respond immediately to new inquiries
- qualify leads by group size, budget, and event type
- automate follow-up until booked or closed
- improve deposit and reminder workflows

### Gift Cards And Retention

- improve gift card redemption
- convert recipients into repeat guests
- trigger referral and review campaigns

### Reporting

- track where revenue actually comes from
- measure conversion by bookings, lessons, memberships, leagues, and events
- identify drop-off points
- compare performance across future locations

## Why This Matters Financially

The value is straightforward:

- fewer lost leads
- fewer no-shows
- higher lesson completion
- stronger membership retention
- better event close rates
- more repeat bookings
- better visibility into what drives revenue

In other words:

> The FlōGolf System would not just make operations cleaner. It would increase revenue by reducing leakage across the customer lifecycle.

## Recommended Positioning

This is the language to use:

> Right now, parts of FlōGolf appear fragmented across booking tools, event software, manual lesson scheduling, membership activation, and separate website flows. I can consolidate that into the FlōGolf System: one internal operating layer that manages the full customer lifecycle from first booking through memberships, lessons, leagues, events, and retention. That gives you more revenue, fewer lost customers, less manual work, full control over your workflows and data, and a system you can scale across multiple locations.

Shorter version:

> I’d build FlōGolf’s internal system so you stop losing customers between disconnected tools and manual processes. Instead of running bookings, lessons, memberships, leagues, events, and follow-up across separate systems, everything connects under one operating layer built for revenue, retention, and multi-location scale.

## Important Strategic Framing

Do not pitch this as "replace everything immediately."

Pitch it as:

1. centralize customer data
2. centralize workflows
3. centralize reporting
4. integrate with the current tools where needed
5. phase out weak points over time

That framing is stronger because it sounds operationally realistic.
